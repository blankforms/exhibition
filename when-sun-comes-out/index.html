Ketuta Alexi-Meskhishvili (1979) is a Georgian photographer based in Berlin, Germany. She grew up in New York, where she studied the practice at Bard College. Alexi-Meskhishvili’s work converges the boundaries between analog and digital photography. Her recent solo exhibitions include ‘Hollow Body’ at the Andrea Rosen Gallery, New York, and Kölnischer Kunstverein, Cologne.

Kai Althoff
2018
Oil in linen
26in x 23in; 36.25in x 32.75in
Kai Althoff (1966) is a German multimedia contemporary artist living and working in Cologne. His work often explores German folklore and everyday life, and elements of German expressionism. Althoff’s art has been exhibited in The Museum of Modern Art in New York and the ICA Boston.

Leilah Babirye (1985) is a Ugandan multidisciplinary artist based in New York City. After being outed for her Queer identity in Uganda, the artist sought and was granted asylum. Her abstract sculptures put topics of sexuality and human rights into conversation. Babirye had solo exhibits at Rebecca Camacho Presents in San Francisco and Gordon Robichaux in New York. The Trapholt Museum, Kolding, Denmark, and the FIAR: Fire Island Residency Exhibition in Long Island, NY also featured her work in group exhibitions.
